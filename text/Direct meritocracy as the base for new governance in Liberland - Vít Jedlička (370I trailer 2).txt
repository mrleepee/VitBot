SUMMARY
A trailer for the "What is the Future for Cities" podcast episode 370 featuring Vít Jedlička, president of Liberland. He introduces Liberland's "direct meritocracy" governance model, explaining how it differs from Singapore's meritocracy by incorporating corporate shareholding elements into society while maintaining democratic checks. Citizens can delegate their voting power to representatives or vote directly with their merits (shares).

KEY POINTS
- Liberland's system is called "direct meritocracy" — citizens hold merits (shares) and can either delegate voting power to representatives or vote directly
- Differs from Singapore's meritocracy: Liberland incorporates corporate shareholding elements into governance rather than rewarding government service
- Democratic check: majority of citizens can veto any rule or person in government they don't like
- Democracy is limited: the majority cannot make decisions on behalf of the minority or redistribute wealth
- Decisions in Liberland are made by majority of shareholders/shares, not simply majority of citizens
