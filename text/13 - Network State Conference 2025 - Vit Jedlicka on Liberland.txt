SUMMARY
Vít Jedlička presents Liberland at the 2025 Network State Conference, marking 10 years since its founding. He outlines how Liberland meets the Montevideo criteria for statehood, describes its blockchain-based governance system combining meritocracy, republic, democracy, and monarchy elements, and announces recent milestones including the election of Justin Sun as prime minister and Britannica Kaiser to Congress. He invites attendees to upcoming events including a longevity conference, tree house construction in Liberland, and a constitutional convention in Prague.

KEY POINTS
- Liberland founded April 13, 2015 on unclaimed land between Croatia and Serbia; nearly 1 million citizenship sign-ups
- 7 square kilometers (3x Monaco), 1,300 citizens from ~800,000 applications, representation in 90+ countries
- Meets Montevideo criteria: permanent population (2+ years), defined territory, government (first decentralized autonomous government/DAO), diplomatic capacity
- Blockchain governance built in Rust combining meritocracy (corporate decisions), republic (limited government scope), democracy (citizen veto power), and monarchy (founder veto powers)
- Justin Sun elected as prime minister (wealthiest PM ever elected); Britannica Kaiser also elected to Congress
- Recent elections held 3 days prior; elections occur every 3 months with 21 Congress members
- Upcoming: tree house construction project in Liberland, longevity clinic in Serbia, constitutional convention in Prague (Dec 19) with Herman Hoppe and Stefan Kinsella
- $40M+ regional investment planned; improving relations with Croatia
