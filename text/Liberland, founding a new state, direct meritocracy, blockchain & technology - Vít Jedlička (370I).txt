SUMMARY
In-depth podcast interview (Episode 370 of "What is the Future for Cities") with Vít Jedlička covering the full story of Liberland's founding, its governance philosophy, blockchain technology, economic model, diplomatic strategy, and future vision. Jedlička explains why he started a new country rather than reforming Czech Republic, how the "direct meritocracy" system works in detail, the role of blockchain in eliminating bureaucracy, the merit-based justice system, plans for AI integration including AI citizens, competition in security/justice/diplomacy, and partnerships with special economic zones. The conversation also covers citizenship utility, longevity initiatives, and the upcoming constitutional convention.

KEY POINTS

Founding & Motivation:
- Started April 13, 2015 (Thomas Jefferson's birthday) on unclaimed land between Croatia and Serbia
- Motivated by frustration with high taxes (55-60% in Czech Republic) and over-regulation in Europe
- Received more immigration requests than the US in first two weeks; ~800,000+ formal applications, likely 1.2-1.3M total including unprocessed emails
- Inspired by Frédéric Bastiat's writings on the state as robber; Czech Republic went from 6x richer than Singapore to 4x poorer in 80 years due to policies

Governance — "Direct Meritocracy":
- Four pillars: meritocracy (corporate shareholding decisions), republic (government limited to security/justice/diplomacy), democracy (citizen veto only — cannot create new laws), monarchy (Senate of founders/top contributors)
- Congress: meritocratic elected body, only institution that can make new laws; 3 institutions can veto laws
- Senate: 100 seats for biggest contributors; elects president every 5 years; president has mainly ceremonial/veto powers
- Justin Sun elected PM (wealthiest PM ever); personal income ~$30M/day; could finance entire city construction
- Inspired by UK House of Lords, Liechtenstein sovereignty, Singapore meritocracy, US republicanism

Blockchain & Technology:
- Custom Rust-based blockchain; 81 decentralized nodes run by citizens
- All governance runs as smart contracts: elections, budgeting, company registration, justice, property transfers
- Elections cost ~$2 total for entire society vs. billions in traditional countries; results in 1 second
- Completely transparent state budget — first country to achieve this
- EVM compatible — can run Ethereum smart contracts
- Removes ~90% of bureaucracy; estimated 15-20% annual economic growth potential

Merit System & Economics:
- $2 contribution = 1 merit; merits are staked (can unstake 10% per year); tradable on exchanges (without voting power)
- 70 million merits total; 10 merits = 1 square meter of land
- Merits serve as: voting power, land rights, and justice collateral
- Voluntary taxes only — contributions earn shares rather than being extracted
- Merits can be seized to compensate victims in justice disputes

Justice System:
- AI-assisted first round of dispute resolution
- If rejected: each party picks a judge, two judges pick a third; 2-of-3 decision via smart contract
- Appeals possible but more expensive at each level
- Merit collateral enables immediate partial compensation (merits unstaked from offender to victim)

AI Integration:
- AI can become full Liberland citizen if it passes KYC; can own property, vote, run for Congress
- Planning AI assistants for citizens: daily personalized advice on voting, contributing, running for office
- Vision: brain-scanning technology in ~10 years to create AI agents that represent individuals eternally

Diplomacy & International:
- Partnerships with special economic zones (e.g., Tanzania recognizes Liberland companies)
- Offering blockchain governance technology to other countries for free trade zones
- Relations with Liberia, El Salvador, and others
- "Silent diplomacy" — working with states without publishing successes
- Liberland passport usable in 20-30 countries (Africa, Oceania, parts of Central/South America)

Security:
- Uberized security model: app-based, anyone can respond to incidents and get paid
- Works globally, not just in Liberland; security agencies can plug in
- Same app used for transparent state procurement/tenders

Upcoming:
- Constitutional convention Dec 19 in Prague with Herman Hoppe, Stefan Kinsella, Alexandra Fusilo
- Longevity conference Nov 3-4 at the Ark; BBC coverage
- Free Private Cities conference Nov 1-2 in Prague
